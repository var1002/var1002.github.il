Pharmacy Programs: Pre-Requisites

What are pre-requisites?

Pre-requisites are classes that need to be completed before applying for a pharmacy program. The are required classes needed as a baseline. Some schools may require you just to have these pre-requisite classes and other may want for you to have recieved your bachelor's degree and have taken these classes. Most school's have very similar requirements.

List of pre-requisites with a Bachelor's Degree:

Here is a sample list of pre-requisites from the Massachusetts College of Pharmacy and Health Sciences.

  • General Biology I & II with lab (8 credits)
  • General Chemistry I & II with lab (8 credits)
  • Microbiology with lab (3 credits)
  • Organic Chemistry I & II with lab (8 credits)
  • Calculus (3 credits)
  • Math or Computer Science Elective (3 credits)
  • Statistics (3 credits)
  • Physics with lab (3 credits)

List of Pre-Requisites without a Bachelor's Degree

A sample list from Massachusetts College of Pharmacy and Health Sciences

  • English Composition (3 credits)
  • English Elective (3 credits)
  • Introduction to Psychology (3 credits)
  • Introduction to Sociology (3 credits)
  • Introduction to History or Political Science (3 credits)
  • Economics (3 credits)
  • Humanities elective (3 credits)
  • Social Science Elective (3 credits)
  • Behavioral Science elective (3 credits)
