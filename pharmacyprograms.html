Pharmacy Programs

Pharmacy school is a graduate school, so going in you can expect it to be harder than school prior. A pharmacy program is used to expand your knowledge in the pharmacy field and the classes accompany this knowledge. Also, in a pharmacy program most of your last year consists of clinicals and internships. Clinicals and Internships are provided for you to give you a sense of what the job as a pharmacist would be like and to give you hands on experience. In the program you can expect to take advanced classes, have clinicals, internships, and labs pretaining to the classes you are taking.

How to Apply?

Applying to pharmacy schools can be different for some schools, but most have you use PharmCAS. PharmCAS is the pharmacy school application process online. With this online application process you create a universal application that can be sent to any pharmacy school you would like to apply to, and shows you the options of which schools use this service. The process is as simple as going to the site, creating an account, and filling out your personal information. It also asks for your transcript, refrences, extracurricular activities, and other activies such as a job.

How do you know if pharmacy is right for you?

Although it may seem like a daunting career choice, it is still a rewarding career. Pharmacy isn't just counting pills, it is the first line of healthcare. Pharmacists provide patients with effective medications and expert advise on how to use them to help thier health and well-being. As a pharmacist you don't always need to work at a retail store. You can work at a hospital as both a pharmacist and a clinical pharmacist. There are lots of ways to go about the pharmacy field.
